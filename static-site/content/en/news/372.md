# February 2026 Blog (Japanese Translation)

Uh... just... let me catch my breath for a second... just a little longer... 😮‍💨

Hi everyone! February was really hard. I worked nonstop, writing and reviewing over and over, and we hit a record high for the number of developer tutorials produced!

But it's not over yet. In other words, before I can return to that beloved city of mine... yes, that one... this pace will continue a little longer.

As a result, this report is quite plain. Only plots, no screenshots. I know, I can hear the booing.

## Summary of What We Did

- **Content Writing**
**Developer Page** (task-docs-dev): We created tutorials about [creating](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/accounts/configure-multisig/) and [signing](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/accounts/sign-multisig/) multisig accounts, [transaction sponsorship (gas fee delegation)](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/transactions/fee-sponsorship/), and [adding account restrictions](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/accounts/account-restrictions/).

  **Developer Page** (task-docs-dev): We added a [serialization table](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/reference/serialization/) and [WebSocket API](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/reference/websockets/) to the reference guide.

  **Textbook Page** (task-docs-textbook): We added a [metadata](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/textbook/metadata/) page to the textbook.
- **Review**
**Developer Page** (task-docs-dev):
[Zero](https://github.com/zero4862) created new tutorials on how to link namespaces to [addresses](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/namespaces/link-namespace-to-address/) and [mosaics](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/namespaces/link-namespace-to-mosaic/), how to add metadata to [accounts](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/namespaces/namespace-metadata/), [namespaces](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/namespaces/namespace-metadata/), and [mosaics](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/mosaics/mosaic-metadata/), plus [creating mosaics](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/mosaics/create-mosaic/), [updating mosaic definitions](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/mosaics/modify-mosaic-definition/), [namespace receipts](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/namespaces/resolve-namespace-from-receipt/), [mosaic revocation](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/mosaics/revoke-mosaic/), and [chain state](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/chain/chain-heights/). All were reviewed and published.

  **NEM**: [CryptoBeliever](https://github.com/cryptoBeliever) did an amazing job writing a complete [OpenAPI](https://www.openapis.org/) specification for the [NIS1 REST API](https://github.com/NemProject/nem/tree/dev/openapi). It totals 180 files and more than 4,500 lines of YAML, and required weeks of review exchanges. This will be used for the upcoming new NEM documentation.

  Now I just need to convince him to do the same for Symbol's REST API. I think the [current documentation](https://docs.symboltest.net/en/devbook/reference/rest/symbol/) can still be improved a lot 😊
- **Management**
I created a roadmap for developing the new NEM documentation site and received art direction from the art team.

  For one month, two writers will work full-time on tutorial production, so we needed time to prepare task lists and job batching.

This is the current status of all documentation tasks.

As you can see, we made major progress in many areas! If the overall Symbol Documentation bar doesn't seem to move, it's because I keep adding tasks there, and that temporarily pushes progress backward. Once those tasks are completed, the top bar should jump forward significantly. 😁

So that was February: work, work, and more work, with no fun at all. Which means no XYM City either. But actually, this was good for the game! Taking a bit of distance let me review it with a fresh perspective. When I return to XYM City, I'll come back with explosive energy and a little more to talk about there.

## What's Next

Zero and I will continue the Symbol documentation sprint until the tutorials are completed.

After that, he will move on to the new NEM documentation, and I will go back to reviews... then work on XYM City.

Also, the new Symbol wallet is progressing smoothly, and I will write guides as soon as it is complete. These are easier than developer tutorials.

I'm not out yet, but I can see the light at the end of the tunnel! And it's neon-colored. 😎

Thank you for reading this far. See you again next month!
